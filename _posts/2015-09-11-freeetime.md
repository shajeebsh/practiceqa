It **snows** in Alaska. I **watch** television everyday. I **visit** my cousin all the time. ## 2) PROGRESSIVE VERB TENSES

[![Present Progressive Tense](image/png;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAANAAAACHAQMAAABd4UV4AAAABlBMVEUAAAAAAAClZ7nPAAAAAXRSTlMAQObYZgAAAEpJREFUSMdjQAZMKLxRqVGpUalRqVGpUSmaSFkV4JSSe4BTiv8ATin2BhTuf5wATRceu8hxoVXBoAjeUalRqVGpUalRqVEp2kgBAMz8IN9rbI+/AAAAAElFTkSuQmCC)](<http://www.grammarbank.com/present-continuous-tense.html>)

### Present Progressive Tense

He **is sleeping** right now. He is asleep at the moment. His sleep _is in progress at the present time_ , and will probably continue.(at least for a while) See [Present Simple vs Present Continuous](<http://www.grammarbank.com/present-continuous-tense.html>) for detailed explanations. **PDFs:** [Positive](<http://www.grammarbank.com/support-files/present-continuous-positive.pdf>)   [Negative & Questions](<http://www.grammarbank.com/support-files/present-continuous-negative-questions.pdf>)   [Simple Present vs Continuous 1](<http://www.grammarbank.com/support-files/present-simple-vs-present-continuous-worksheet1.pdf>)   [2](<http://www.grammarbank.com/support-files/present-simple-vs-present-continuous-worksheet2.pdf>)

### Past Progressive Tense

He **was sleeping** when I arrived. He went to sleep at 10:00 last night. I arrived at 11:00. He was still asleep. His sleep began before and _was in progress at a particular time in the past_. It probably continued.(at least for a while) See [Past Continuous Tense](<http://www.grammarbank.com/past-continuous-tense.html>) for detailed explanations. **PDFs:** [Past Simple vs Past Continuous Worksheet 1](<http://www.grammarbank.com/support-files/past-simple-vs-past-continuous-worksheet-1.pdf>)   [Worksheet 2](<http://www.grammarbank.com/support-files/past-simple-vs-past-continuous-worksheet-2.pdf>)

### Future Progressive Tense

He **will be sleeping** when we arrive. He will go to sleep at 10:00 tomorrow night. We will arrive around 11:00. The action of sleeping will begin before we arrive, and it _will be in progress at a particular time in the future_. His sleep will probably continue.(at least for a while) See [Future Continuous Tense](<http://www.grammarbank.com/future-continuous-tense.html>) for details.

Fill-In

Complete each sentence with a quantifier that you've just learned.

 

1. Kimberly has met with the consultant only  times, once in Rome and twice in New York.

Explanation

**Correct Answers:  **

a few

**Explanation:** The answers above are correct. _Times_  is a countable noun, so _a few_  can be used with it to mean a small number.

 

2. I've flown back and forth from San Francisco to London twelve times this year, so I have  frequent flyer miles.

Explanation

**Correct Answers:  **

a lot of

many

**Explanation:** The answers above are correct. _Miles_  is a countable noun, so _a lot of_  and _many_  can be used with it to mean a large number.

 

3. This trip has been almost perfect. There have been  problems.

Explanation

**Correct Answers:  **

few

**Explanation:** The answer above is correct. _Problems_  is a countable noun, so _few_  can be used to mean almost none. Do not confuse _few_  with _a few_.

 

4. There wasn't  space in Michael's office to hold the meeting, so we used the conference room instead.

Explanation

**Correct Answers:  **

enough

**Explanation:** The answer above is correct. _Space_  is an uncountable noun, so _enough_  can be used with it to mean as much as necessary.

 

5. The second person I interviewed for the bank manager position doesn't have  experience. He has worked in banking for only three months.

Explanation

**Correct Answers:  **

a lot of

a great deal of

much

enough

**Explanation:** The answers above are correct. _Experience_  is an uncountable noun, so _a lot of, a great deal of_ , and _much_  can be used with it to mean a large amount. _Enough_  can be used with it to mean as much as necessary.

 

6. We need to buy paper for the copy machine. I looked for some in the cabinet, but I didn't see .

Explanation

**Correct Answers:  **

any

**Explanation:** The answer above is correct. _Paper_  can be an uncountable noun, so _any_  can be used in this negative statement to mean even the smallest amount of paper.

 

7. Anna called  investors today, but I'm not sure how many.

Explanation

**Correct Answers:  **

some

several

a few

a lot of

many

**Explanation:** The answers above are correct. _Investors_  is a countable noun, so _some_ , _several_ , _a few_ , _a lot of_ , and _many_  can be used with it to mean a medium to large number.

 

8. Dianne will change one or two things when she starts to head the department, but she won't make  changes.

Explanation

**Correct Answers:  **

many

a lot of

**Explanation:** The answers above are correct. _Changes_  is a countable noun, so _a lot of_  and _many_  can be used with it to mean a large number. _Several_  is not usually used in negative sentences.
